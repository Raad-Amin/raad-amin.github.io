About

I'm a high school student in Sylvania, Ohio, and just about everything I do traces back to the same habit: noticing when something's broken, and then trying to do something about it (hopefully fix it, that is). A deeply flawed AI detection system creating false accusations willy-nilly, a gap in free, quality notes, or something that's just plain boring and could use some renovation.

That's produced a 36-page academic honesty defense, a district-wide guide on fighting positives from erroneous AI detectors, an open-licensed notes program used by over 100 students, and a couple of editorials that ruffled a few feathers. Besides that, I'm also a NOAA/NWS-certified SKYWARN severe weather observer, and a few years into a meteophotography archive documenting atmospheric events and cloud formations.

Extracurricular-wise, I try to keep myself busy. I've competed in Science Olympiad and Quizbowl since the 6th grade alongside Speech & Debate, Student Government, Powerlifting, and far more Wikipedia edits than intended. I don't consider myself a writer, more of a hater, piecing out flaws and holes in the system to bring awareness and resolve them. Whether through reporting, satire, archival, or personal advocacy, I strive to make sure things work.

District Seeks First Levy Since 2016 Amid $4 Million Deficit

Low per-pupil spending, tens of millions in reserve, and a AA credit rating might on paper sound like ideal finances for a school district, but a combination of hostile legislation — both 50 years old and modern — alongside sharply increasing prices created the perfect storm pulling Sylvania Schools' budget apart.
