Science Olympiad Website

Status

Actively maintained

Team record

5× Regional Finalist, 2× State, 1× State Finalist

Audiences served

Team members, parents, visiting teams at hosted invitational

Content

Competition schedules, results, FAQ, photo gallery, yearbook excerpts, news briefs, invitational logistics

Purpose

Modernization; polished public face; centralized external coordination

As part of our Division C team's continual modernization efforts, I personally built and maintained the Science Olympiad team's official website. The site includes competition schedules, results, FAQ for new members and their families, a team photo gallery, yearbook excerpts, news briefs, and resources for teams attending the invitational we host. This streamlined external coordination with parents and other teams while presenting a more polished public face for the team.

Science Olympiad Yearbook

Tools

Adobe InDesign, Photoshop

Format

Physical print; yearly-changing thematic design

Content

Event highlights, results, team photos, build nights, practices, competition & travel

Contribution model

Custom layouts + team member submissions each year

Purpose

Memory preservation; team identity & morale

Each year, I curate, design, and publish a physical yearbook with Adobe InDesign and Photoshop for our Science Olympiad team. It includes event highlights, results, team photos, and behind-the-scenes moments from build nights, practices, competitions, and travel. Beyond preserving memories, the yearbook helps build team identity and morale, helping represent the team as a whole. I use custom layouts, thematic design, and contributions from team members to make each edition a reflection of our shared experience that year.
