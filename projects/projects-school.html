F.R. LLC Noteshare Program

Status

Active & growing

Students reached

100+

License

CC BY-NC-SA 4.0

Subjects

AP World, APUSH, AP Gov, AP Seminar, Hon. English 9 & 10, Hon. Physical Science, Hon. Biology

Collaboration model

Solo, invite-only, or fully open — depending on course

Distribution

Free, publicly shared — no private tiers

Named after an old inside joke, the F.R. LLC Noteshare Program is my foremost way of giving back to my school and my peers. It's simple in premise, but large in scope. I structure, organize, and maintain top-quality notes for a variety of intensive Honors and AP courses which I've taken. Depending on the course, I either grow the notes entirely myself, invite trusted collaborators to help me, or — in some cases — open the notes for anyone to add to and edit, relying on the goodwill of my peers to grow them in extraordinary detail which I couldn't achieve alone.

Presently, my notes have been used by over 100 students across various grade levels spanning AP World History, AP US History, AP Government, AP Seminar, Honors English 9 and 10, Honors Physical Science, and Honors Biology. They've been described as "lifesavers," and many of my classmates credit my notes and accompanying breakdowns with markedly higher test scores.

I don't keep any "private" notes of differing quality — all the notes I take are a part of the program, free for anyone to share and use. All notes are licensed under the Creative Commons Attribution-NonCommercial-ShareAlike 4.0 International license, meaning anyone can adapt, modify, and share them as long as they don't sell it, give credit, and share any derivatives under the same license.

How To Write an Academic Honesty Defense

Origin

36-page personal defense, compiled overnight

Peers supported

6 false-positive cases cleared

Evidence types

Google Docs & Grammarly edit histories, competing AI detector results, algorithmic analysis

Distribution

District-wide; cornerstone of Noteshare Program

Guide covers

Tone, evidence collection, student rights, writing style explanation

After being falsely flagged for AI use on my first assignment of high school, I was offered the opportunity to rewrite the essay, no questions asked. Instead, I decided to write a 36-page report detailing how and why my essay was my own product. I spent the entire afternoon, evening, and night of the first day compiling various sources — Google Docs and Grammarly edit histories — and utilized a variety of other AI detection tools to prove the result found by Copyleaks, the detector purchased by my district, was not only an erroneous false positive, but almost random when assigning guilt. That process was stressful, but fun, though I doubt others would find it as thrilling.

Subsequently clearing my accusation and supporting 6 of my peers who found themselves in the same situation, I decided to create a clear guide to help others navigate it. I wrote an accessible breakdown of how to structure an academic honesty defense: what tone to use, what evidence to gather, what rights you have, and how to explain personal writing choices without sounding defensive. It's been publicly shared with every student in the district and is a cornerstone of the Noteshare Program's academic resources.
