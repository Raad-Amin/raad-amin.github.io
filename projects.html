Project 01

F.R. LLC Noteshare Program

Aug 2025 – Present

Open Access CC BY-NC-SA 4.0 Collaborative 100+ Students

There was a gap in accessible, high-quality study materials at Northview — notes either didn't exist, were gatekept, or weren't structured well enough to be useful. The F.R. LLC Noteshare Program was built to fix that.

The program distributes structured, well-organized notes across AP and Honors courses: AP World History, AP US History, AP Government, AP Seminar, Honors English, Honors Biology, and others. Every document is licensed CC BY-NC-SA 4.0 — free to use, adapt, and redistribute for non-commercial purposes, as long as credit is preserved and any derivative work carries the same license.

How it works

Notes are collaboratively maintained and publicly shared. Nothing is paywalled, nothing requires sign-up. Over 100 students across grade levels currently use the materials. The goal has always been parity of access: a student who missed a class, is catching up after illness, or just learns better from a second pass at structured material shouldn't be at a disadvantage.

Scope

Materials are organized by course, then by unit and topic. Each document includes key vocabulary, structured summaries of primary source material, and exam-relevant frameworks. The program is updated as coursework progresses and expanded as additional contributors join.

Project 02

Academic Honesty Defense Guide

Apr 2025

Student Advocacy Policy Investigative District-Wide Distribution

When a student is accused of AI-assisted writing, the burden of proof in practice falls on them — even when no written policy establishes that, and even when the detection tool in question produces false positives at a significant rate. Most students have no idea how to respond.

This guide was built after going through that process firsthand. The original defense ran 36 pages. This document distills the most transferable knowledge into a format any student could use, regardless of whether they know what perplexity scoring is or how transformer models tokenize text.

What it covers

The guide walks through the full response process: what evidence to gather immediately, how to document your writing process retroactively, how to frame your defense without inadvertently conceding the premise of the accusation, and what rights students have under school policy and Ohio law. It also explains — in plain terms — why AI detection tools are unreliable, and how to use that technical reality in your defense without requiring a computer science background.

Distribution

Shared district-wide following the publication of the Copyleaks investigation in The Student Prints. Six peers have used versions of this framework to successfully resolve false-positive accusations. The guide is publicly available and free to adapt.

Related

This guide grew directly out of the investigative editorial on Copyleaks published in The Student Prints. See The District's AI Detector Doesn't Work for the full investigation.

Project 03

Team Websites

Ongoing

Web Development Design Content Management

Built and maintain the official websites for three Northview co-curricular programs: Science Olympiad, Speech & Debate, and The Student Prints. Each site serves a different audience with different needs, so each has a different structure — but all share the same underlying goal: make the team's work visible and the information easy to find.

Science Olympiad

Competition schedule and results archive, team roster, event breakdown by division, build night documentation, and resources for incoming members. Updated after each invitational and regional. Also houses coordination materials for events Northview hosts.

Speech & Debate

Tournament schedule, individual and team results, judge information, and resources for each event format. Designed to be useful for both current members navigating the season and prospective members deciding whether to join.

The Student Prints

Article archive, staff directory, submission guidelines, and contact information. Built to give the paper a permanent home outside of print runs and social media, and to preserve past issues in a searchable format.

Project 04

Science Olympiad Yearbook

Annual

Adobe InDesign Adobe Photoshop Editorial Design Print

An annual physical yearbook documenting the Science Olympiad season from first practice to final invitational. Designed entirely in Adobe InDesign with photo editing in Photoshop.

Each edition covers the full arc of the season: team roster, event assignments, build night documentation, invitational results, state competition coverage, and behind-the-scenes photographs. The design changes year to year — each edition gets its own thematic direction, color system, and layout logic rather than recycling the previous year's template.

Design approach

The challenge with a team yearbook is balancing documentation with readability. Competition results and rosters need to be scannable; narrative spreads need to hold attention. The layouts use a modular grid that allows both — dense data tables can coexist with full-bleed photography without either feeling cramped or wasted.

Photography is shot throughout the season and edited consistently so that images from different lighting conditions and locations feel coherent when placed together on a spread.

Project 05

Meteophotography Archive

Ongoing

SKYWARN Certified NOAA / NWS Public Domain Unsplash

NOAA-certified SKYWARN severe weather observer since May 2024. The certification involves training in identifying and reporting severe weather accurately — storm structure, precipitation types, wind, hail, and tornado indicators — and submitting standardized spotter reports to the National Weather Service during active events.

The meteophotography archive is a parallel, ongoing project: a field-logged collection of atmospheric and weather photography documenting what that observation looks like in practice. Entries include severe convective events, notable cloud formations (mammatus, arcus, undulatus, asperitas), atmospheric optical phenomena, and significant weather days in northwest Ohio and the surrounding region.

Field documentation

Each image is logged with timestamp, location coordinates, atmospheric conditions at the time of capture, and any associated SKYWARN report if the event met reporting thresholds. The archive functions as both a personal record and a visual catalog of regional weather patterns over time.

Public domain releases

Select images from the archive have been released to the public domain via Unsplash. These are available for any use, without attribution required, though credit is appreciated. Images are chosen for release based on technical quality and meteorological significance.
